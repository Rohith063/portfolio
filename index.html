🤖 ML + Graph Theory · Published Research

Hyderabad Metro Route Optimizer & Fare Predictor

📄

Published Research Paper

This project was written up and accepted as a peer-reviewed paper in the Journal of Object Technology (JOT). Co-authored with Dharani Kommoji, Koturi Gnanendra, H Hima Giri, and two faculty advisors from HITS.

A hybrid framework that models the entire Hyderabad Metro Rail network — 64 stations across 3 corridors — as a weighted graph, runs Dijkstra's algorithm to find the optimal route between any two stations, and feeds the resulting travel distance into a Decision Tree Regressor trained on official HMRL fare slab data to predict the exact fare. Both outputs are served together through a Flask web application in real time.

⚡ Results: Sub-millisecond route computation (mean 0.58ms across 50 test cases). Decision Tree achieved MAE = 0.0 and R² = 1.0 on fare prediction — outperforming Linear Regression (MAE = 3.45) and Random Forest. End-to-end response time averaged 62ms including browser rendering.
🎯 Why it matters: One of very few student-built end-to-end ML tools applied to real Indian urban transit infrastructure. Addresses information asymmetry faced by migrants, students and first-time metro users who lack route and fare awareness.

🎙️ Voice AI · Chess Engine · Side Project

Voice Chess AI — Play Chess with Your Voice

A full-stack chess application where you speak your move in natural language and Stockfish — one of the strongest chess engines in the world — responds and announces its move aloud. Built with Whisper for offline speech-to-text, pyttsx3 for voice output, WebSockets for real-time play, and a custom NLP move parser that understands phrases like "knight to f3" or "castle kingside" and converts them to UCI notation.

⚡ Full working app — voice in, voice out, click-to-move board, legal move validation, undo support, adjustable Stockfish skill levels (1–20). Runs locally via FastAPI + WebSocket backend with a single-file HTML frontend.
